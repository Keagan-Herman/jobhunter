# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo job-seeker using this daily: scanning new matches, reviewing AI scores, drafting cover letters, tracking applications. Occasionally shared with others (friends, recruiters) — so the interface needs to read cleanly to a stranger, not just to its creator.

## Product Purpose

JobHunter is a personal AI co-pilot for the job search. It fetches, scores, and surfaces the most relevant roles so the user spends time on applications that matter, not on noise. Success means: open the dashboard, instantly see what's worth your attention, act on it, close the tab.

## Brand Personality

Premium, polished, confident. The UI should feel like a tool worth paying for — tight, intentional, fast. Reference: Raycast. The specific quality that matters is that every interaction feels snappy and deliberate: no decoration that doesn't carry information, no motion that doesn't signal state.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS cream**: warm-neutral backgrounds, soft rounded type, "streamline your workflow" copy.
- **Cyberpunk over-glow**: too many neon layers, excessive blur, looks like a gaming overlay. The green accent is intentional — but it should punctuate, not saturate.
- **Corporate enterprise gray**: lifeless neutral palette, zero personality, feels like an internal HR tool.

## Design Principles

1. **Signal over decoration** — every visual element earns its place by carrying information or confirming state. Glows and motion answer "what changed?" not "how cool is this?"
2. **Scan-first hierarchy** — a user should know their top 3 matches within two seconds of opening the dashboard, without reading.
3. **Raycast precision** — tight type, deliberate whitespace, responsive to keyboard and pointer alike. The design respects the user's time.
4. **Accent as punctuation** — the electric green (`#00ff87`) is the signal color. It marks scores, active states, and confirmations. Diluting it with too many secondary accents reduces its meaning.
5. **Sharable confidence** — the interface should feel credible to someone seeing it for the first time. No insider-only aesthetics that require context to appreciate.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. `prefers-reduced-motion` support for all animations (currently absent — priority fix). Sufficient contrast on all text against dark backgrounds. Keyboard navigability for primary actions (scan, apply, skip).
